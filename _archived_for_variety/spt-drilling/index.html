SPT (Standard Penetration Test) Services in Eugene, Oregon

Geotechnical engineering with regional judgment.

LEARN MORE

When ASTM D1586 drives the subsurface investigation, the Standard Penetration Test remains the benchmark for obtaining both a disturbed sample and an in-situ resistance value in one operation. In Eugene, where the Willamette River and its tributaries have deposited sequences of silts, sands, and organic clays across the valley floor, interpreting SPT blow counts correctly matters. The city sits at roughly 430 feet above sea level, with groundwater often encountered within 10 to 15 feet. That shallow water table combined with Holocene alluvium creates conditions where standard drilling without careful SPT energy calibration can mislead the geotechnical model. Our field crews operate CME-75 and similar automatic trip hammers with real-time energy monitoring, ensuring that N60 values presented in the log are corrected per Seed and Idriss methodology. For projects in the Bethel-Danebo area or near the McKenzie River confluence, where loose saturated sands appear, we frequently pair SPT data with liquefaction analysis to assess the potential for cyclic mobility under the Cascadia design event.

An SPT N-value alone is just a number. Corrected for energy, overburden, and rod length against a site-specific stratigraphic column, it becomes the foundation of a defensible geotechnical design.

Methodology and scope

Eugene's population has grown steadily past 175,000, pushing residential and commercial development onto terrain that was historically bypassed. The 1993 Scotts Mills earthquake, though centered north of the city, reminded local engineers that moderate-magnitude crustal events can still generate site amplification in the basin. SPT refusal or high N-values in the Eugene Formation's dense gravels, typically encountered on the southern slopes toward Spencer Butte, contrast sharply with the N-values of 4 to 10 found in the floodplain alluvium. Our laboratory, accredited to ISO 17025 for soil classification, processes the split-spoon samples through grain-size analysis and Atterberg limits within 48 hours of drilling. This rapid turnaround allows the design team to reconcile field blow counts with laboratory index properties before finalizing bearing capacity recommendations. For sites where the upper 20 feet show erratic SPT profiles, we may recommend supplemental CPT soundings to capture a continuous tip resistance and sleeve friction trace without the disturbance inherent in the drive-and-sample process.

Local considerations

Eugene's urban fabric expanded rapidly after World War II, with neighborhoods like the Whiteaker and River Road areas built on fill and alluvial deposits long before modern geotechnical standards existed. Skip a proper SPT investigation on a commercial infill lot there, and the project absorbs a hidden liability. Loose sands at depths of 5 to 20 feet can densify during seismic shaking, leading to settlement or bearing failure that no amount of structural overdesign can mitigate. An SPT boring with continuous sampling through the critical zone provides the N-values needed to run a simplified liquefaction triggering analysis, as outlined by the NCEER workshop recommendations. Without that data, the structural engineer is designing blind. Beyond seismic concerns, encountering undocumented fill with N-values under 3 can mean the difference between a straightforward shallow footing design and an unexpected switch to deep foundation elements that alter the project budget and schedule substantially.

Frequently asked questions

How much does an SPT boring cost in Eugene?

For a typical 30-foot SPT boring with continuous sampling, mobile rig mobilization, and a summary log, the cost ranges from US$510 to US$740 per boring depending on access conditions and the number of borings on the same project. Sites requiring traffic control on busy corridors like Coburg Road or limited-access backyards may fall at the upper end of the range.

How many SPT borings does the City of Eugene require for a commercial building permit?

The City of Eugene Building and Permit Services reviews geotechnical reports under IBC 2021 Section 1803. While the code does not prescribe a fixed number, standard practice for a single-story commercial structure on a half-acre lot is a minimum of two borings. The responsible engineer determines the final count based on the geotechnical variability of the site, the footprint of the structure, and whether the boring layout captures the building corners and any planned stormwater infiltration features.

What depth should an SPT boring reach for a two-story residence in the south Eugene hills?

Borings on the foothills south of 30th Avenue typically extend 20 to 30 feet below the proposed footing elevation, or to refusal in the Eugene Formation's dense cemented gravels. The key is to penetrate any colluvial or weathered zone and demonstrate at least 10 feet of competent bearing material. If the boring encounters bedrock refusal above 20 feet, the report documents the refusal depth and the N-value trend leading to it.
